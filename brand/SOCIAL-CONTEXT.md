# MNENA Social Media Context

**How to use this doc:** paste the whole thing into ChatGPT / Claude / whatever AI you're using, then add your ask at the bottom ("write a caption + image prompt for the August cleanup"). Everything the AI needs to stay on-brand is in here. Written for the Marshall–New Era Neighborhood Association (MNENA), Midtown Sacramento.

---

## Who we are

The Marshall–New Era Neighborhood Association: volunteer neighbors in Midtown Sacramento (roughly the Marshall School / New Era Park area). We run board meetings, quarterly cleanups, a community garden at 26th & B, block parties, National Night Out, and a December holiday party. Our goal: get more neighbors — **renters and homeowners equally** — aware, involved, and connected. Only ~3% of the neighborhood are members; the audience is everyone else.

- Website: marshallnewera.org · Instagram: @marshallnewera · Facebook: @marshallnewera
- Meetings: 3rd Monday, 6pm, Society Church Building, 2711 B St
- Tagline: **"This is our neighborhood. Let's shape it together."**

## Voice

Neighborly, warm, playful-but-clear, proud of Midtown, invitational — never guilt-trippy, never corporate, never hype-y.

| ✅ Sounds like us | ❌ Not like us |
|---|---|
| "Hey neighbor — got plans Saturday?" | "Members are hereby notified of the upcoming general assembly…" |
| "The garden's tomatoes don't pick themselves 🍅" | "🚀✨ UNLOCK your neighborhood's POTENTIAL ✨🚀" |
| "Third Monday, 6pm. Bring a friend, we'll bring the agenda." | "Attendance at cleanup events is strongly encouraged." |

Emojis: 1–3 per post max, used for scannability, not decoration. Contractions always. Sentence case, not Title Case.

## Caption formula

Every post: **Hook → Body → CTA.**

1. **Hook** — first line earns the tap. A question, a date, a tiny joke.
2. **Body** — who / what / when / where in 1–3 short sentences. No walls of text.
3. **CTA** — one clear action: "Save the date", "RSVP via link in bio", "Tag a neighbor", "Reply with your favorite spot".

Hashtags (3–5, at the end): `#NewEraNeighbors #SacramentoLiving #MidtownPride #MidtownSac` + one post-specific.

## Image style — the important part

Our look is **flat vector illustration**: layered silhouettes, no outlines, no gradients, no 3D, no photorealism. A modern take on the mid-century travel poster, looking at the Midtown Sacramento cityscape. Our two hero subjects are the **gold Tower Bridge** and the **historic Marshall School** (gold, American Classical Revival, arched fanlight window) — they anchor our corner of Midtown, with the Capitol dome in the background skyline.

**Reference artwork** lives in the repo at `src/assets/illustrations/` (cityscape, marshall-school, community-garden, homes-bridge) and on the public brand page — attach one to your AI request as a style reference whenever possible; it beats any text description.

**Base image prompt — start from this and adapt:**

> Flat vector illustration, mid-century travel-poster style, no outlines, no gradients. Midtown Sacramento neighborhood scene: [SUBJECT]. Layered composition back to front: muted seafoam-teal sky (#7AB6A4) with cream clouds (#F7ECCC), deep teal downtown skyline silhouette (#004447 / #014D43), layered tree canopies in greens (#5DA145, #368A59, darker teals), Victorian and craftsman homes in cream/gold/teal, gold path or road in foreground (#F3CB4A / #FBDD9F). Palette strictly limited to: #004447, #014D43, #368A59, #5DA145, #479FB3, #7AB6A4, #F7ECCC, #F3CB4A, #EC8A1F, #7CD1E8, #FBDD9F. Greens dominate; the sky is seafoam teal, never cream; gold reserved for landmarks and paths.

**Recognizable motifs** (pick 1–2 per image, don't stack them all):
Tower Bridge in gold · Marshall School with its fanlight arch window · Capitol dome in cream · community garden rows · river waves · tree-lined streets with Victorians · cream clouds

**Variation rules — this is how we stop every post looking the same:**
- Rotate which color leads: teal-dominant one post, cream-dominant the next, gold accent the one after
- Rotate the motif: bridge → garden → school → river → homes
- Rotate the crop: full skyline scene → single building close-up → flat color background with one small illustration + big type
- Max **two** illustration motifs/icons per graphic. Empty space is on-brand.
- Recurring series (board meetings) should share a layout but shuffle the brand colors each month
- Real photos of real neighbors beat illustrations when we have them — candid, daylight, doing things. Warm edit, no heavy filters.

**Never:** clip-art mashups, gradients, neon, stock-photo handshakes, more than two typefaces, white text on bright colors, AI-generated "photos" of fake people presented as neighbors.

## Formats

- Feed post: 1080×1080 (square) or 1080×1350 (portrait, better reach)
- Story/reminder: 1080×1920, big type, one fact, one CTA sticker
- Graphics carry only the essentials (who/what/when/where) — details go in the caption

## Content categories (from our social guide)

| Category | Cadence | Example hook |
|---|---|---|
| Event announcements | each event, ~2 wks out | "🎃 Save the date! Halloween Party Oct 27" |
| Event recaps | within 3 days after | "30+ neighbors showed up Saturday 💪" |
| Calls to action | as needed | "We need 4 volunteers for the Farmers Market booth" |
| Updates & alerts | as needed | "⚠️ E Street closed June 10, 9–4" |
| Story reminders | day-of | "Tonight! Meeting at 6pm 🗓" |
| Behind-the-scenes | monthly-ish | "Printing flyers for NNO like it's 1999" |
| History & pride | monthly-ish | "📸 Marshall & D St, 1978 — recognize anything?" |
| Community spotlights | monthly-ish | "Meet Sandra — bakes for every potluck" |
| User-generated content | when offered, with permission | "Thanks @sacmidtownmom for this shot!" |

Rhythm: ~2 posts/week (Tuesday & Saturday, 10am–2pm), stories for day-of reminders. Mix categories — never two announcement graphics in a row if avoidable.

## Accessibility & housekeeping

- Check comments/DMs for 5–10 min after posting
